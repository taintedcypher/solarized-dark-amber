Palette reconstruction / work document

From resemblance to rationale.

The previous palette looked Solarized-adjacent. The adopted rebuild anchors its amber ramp to measured CIELAB lightness pairs while preserving the character with a restrained accent recalibration.

Adopted decision

The rebuilt base ramp replaces an accidental luminance sequence with deliberate dark pairs at ΔL* 45 and inverse pairs at ΔL* 47.

01 / The case for rebuilding

Solarized’s technical identity is its controlled lightness structure. Reusing eight base names and eight accents establishes lineage; measured, paired contrast makes that lineage defensible.

02 / Side-by-side feel

The rebuild intentionally keeps the same amber atmosphere. The largest changes are functional: base0 becomes viable body text, middle tones separate cleanly, and the pale end becomes parchment rather than bright gold.

03 / Luminance ramp

Each marker is positioned by measured CIELAB L* after conversion to an eight-bit sRGB hex value. The adopted palette follows Solarized’s published target pattern: 15, 20, 45, 50, 60, 65, 92, 97.

04 / Symmetric pair proof

The two dark pairs converge within 0.02 L*, rather than drifting by more than 8 L*. Both also exceed the 4.5:1 WCAG threshold for normal text. The inverse pairs mirror Solarized’s ramp but are documented as structural—not a released light theme.

05 / Base values

The dark backgrounds move slightly lighter, the middle tones gain the missing Solarized-style jumps, and the final two tones lose chroma as they approach white so they remain usable as inverse surfaces.

06 / Accent calibration

The accent hues remain recognizable. Their lightness is grouped deliberately: core warm and purple accents at L* 65, blue at 68, cyan and green at 70, and yellow at 75. Every accent clears 4.5:1 on the adopted background.

07 / Method and gates

Measurements use sRGB relative luminance, CIELAB L* with the D65 sRGB white point, and the WCAG contrast formula. Values shown on this page are calculated from the embedded hex codes rather than typed results.
